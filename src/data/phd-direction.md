# PhD Research Direction

Xiyao Huang is interested in PhD research at the intersection of machine learning, biomedical data analysis, precision medicine, biomarker discovery, disease outcome prediction, biomedical imaging, computer vision, interpretable AI, and efficient AI models.

## Research Themes

- Machine learning for precision medicine: Building models that can support individualized disease understanding and data-driven biomedical research.
- Biomarker discovery: Using interpretable machine learning and feature selection to identify meaningful molecular, imaging, clinical, or behavioral indicators.
- Disease outcome prediction: Developing robust predictive pipelines for biomedical datasets while emphasizing validation, interpretability, and responsible use.
- Biomedical imaging and computer vision: Applying deep learning and efficient visual models to biomedical images and research data.
- Interpretable AI: Designing models and analysis workflows that can provide useful explanations for researchers and domain experts.
- Efficient AI models: Building lightweight or scalable models for large-scale biomedical and computer vision applications.
- Applied AI systems: Translating research models into practical tools for data analysis, decision support, and scientific workflows.

## Fit

Xiyao is especially interested in research groups that combine rigorous machine learning methodology with biomedical relevance, reproducible analysis, and collaboration between computer science and domain experts.
